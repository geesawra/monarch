# Child Safety Standards for Monarch

**Last updated: April 13, 2026**

Monarch is an open-source Android client for the Bluesky social network. We have zero tolerance for child sexual abuse and exploitation (CSAE), including child sexual abuse material (CSAM). This page documents our standards, the moderation model Monarch relies on, and how to report CSAE content or concerns.

## Prohibited content and conduct

The following are strictly prohibited in connection with Monarch:

- Child sexual abuse material (CSAM) of any kind, including real, animated, generated, or illustrated depictions.
- Grooming, sexual extortion, or solicitation of minors.
- Sexualization of minors in text, images, video, or links.
- Trading, linking to, or advertising CSAM or access to it.
- Any other content or conduct that sexually exploits or endangers children.

These standards apply to all content accessed, posted, or shared through Monarch.

## Moderation model

Monarch is a client application. It does not host content. All posts, images, videos, and profiles are fetched from the Bluesky network and its AT Protocol infrastructure.

Content moderation is enforced at multiple layers:

- **Bluesky AppView and PDS operators** remove content that violates the [Bluesky Community Guidelines](https://bsky.social/about/support/community-guidelines), including CSAE, and cooperate with law enforcement and NCMEC as required by law.
- **Bluesky labelers**, including the default Bluesky Moderation Service, tag or hide content that violates safety policies. Monarch respects these labels and hides or warns on flagged content in the client UI.
- **User-level controls** in Monarch allow muting, blocking, and filtering of accounts and content based on labels.
- **Operating system safeguards** (Google Play Protect, Android Safe Browsing) provide an additional layer of defense.

Monarch does not bypass, weaken, or override any of these moderation layers.

## Reporting CSAE content

If you encounter CSAE content through Monarch, please report it immediately.

**Report to Bluesky** — the network that hosts the content:
- In any official Bluesky app, use the built-in report flow.
- Email: [safety@bsky.app](mailto:safety@bsky.app)
- Bluesky safety policies: https://bsky.social/about/support/community-guidelines

**Report to Monarch** — if the concern involves the Monarch app itself, or if you cannot reach Bluesky:
- Email: [hello@geesawra.industries](mailto:hello@geesawra.industries) with subject line `CSAE REPORT`
- GitHub: open a confidential issue at https://github.com/geesawra/monarch/issues (do not include the offending material; describe it and link to the post URI)

We will acknowledge CSAE reports within 48 hours and escalate confirmed cases to Bluesky Trust & Safety and, where legally required, to NCMEC (the U.S. National Center for Missing & Exploited Children) or the competent authority in the reporter's jurisdiction.

**Emergencies** — if a child is in immediate danger, contact local law enforcement first.

- United States: 911, and the NCMEC CyberTipline at https://report.cybertip.org or 1-800-843-5678
- European Union: your national INHOPE hotline at https://inhope.org/EN/hotlines
- United Kingdom: the Internet Watch Foundation at https://www.iwf.org.uk/report

## Compliance

Monarch complies with applicable child safety laws, including:

- 18 U.S.C. § 2258A (reporting of CSAM to NCMEC by electronic service providers, to the extent applicable)
- EU Regulation 2021/1232 and successor legislation on combating online CSAE
- UK Online Safety Act 2023
- Any other applicable local laws in jurisdictions where Monarch is distributed

We cooperate in good faith with lawful requests from law enforcement and child safety authorities.
